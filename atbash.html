Atbash Cipher

Simple substitution cipher reversing the alphabet

How the Atbash Cipher Works

The Atbash cipher is a simple substitution cipher that reverses the alphabet (A ↔ Z, B ↔ Y, etc.).

Encryption/Decryption Process:

  1. Each letter is mapped to its reverse in the alphabet
  2. A becomes Z, B becomes Y, C becomes X, etc.
  3. The same process is used for both encryption and decryption
  4. Non-alphabetic characters are typically left unchanged
